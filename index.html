Ontario Learning Skills

Welcome to Ontario Learning Skills

In Ontario's school system, there are six key skills that are prioritized, not for their direct academic value, but because they help students become successful in all aspects of life. These are the skills that go beyond the textbooks and are listed out in a document known as the "Growing Success" by Ontario's Ministry of Education. The six skills are:

  1. Responsibility
  2. Organization
  3. Independent Work
  4. Collaboration
  5. Initiative
  6. Self-regulation

Learn more about the six essential skills for students' growth and success.

Responsibility is about following through with commitments and understanding that actions have consequences. It's not just about completing homework on time, but developing a broader sense of accountability and self-discipline. This skill teaches learners that they are in control of their actions and must take ownership of the outcomes. This is crucial in real-life situations, such as work or personal relationships, where being reliable and accountable is key.
Organization is about the ability to plan activities, manage time, and use resources efficiently. This skill is more than just keeping a tidy desk; it involves structuring tasks and managing them effectively. Students who develop this skill can better juggle various tasks and commitments, a critical ability in most jobs and personal situations. Just like a project manager balances different aspects of a project, students with good organization skills can navigate their academic and personal life more smoothly.
Independent work is about being able to complete tasks on your own while staying focused. It's about encouraging students to take ownership of their tasks and not always depend on others. This doesn't mean they should never seek help, but it promotes the value of self-reliance. This skill is very useful in various aspects of life, both personal and professional, as it empowers individuals to take charge and manage their tasks, fostering confidence and independence.
Collaboration is about teaching students the ability to work effectively in a team, make positive contributions, respect different opinions, and manage any disagreements in a fair and respectful manner. This isn't just valuable for group school projects, it's a critical skill for the real world too. Whether it's in a professional setting at work or within personal relationships in the community and family, knowing how to cooperate and navigate differences in a constructive way is a key to successful interactions and partnerships.
Initiative refers to the ability to take charge and start tasks without constant reminders or prompts from others. It's about doing more than just what's required, and striving to go the extra mile. In the context of education, it helps students to excel, but it's also an important quality for success in the workplace. It's about being proactive, self-driven and exceeding expectations, which are traits that often set apart high performers in their respective fields.
Self-regulation involves creating personal objectives, monitoring your progress towards these objectives, and understanding when to ask for assistance. It's about evaluating your own performance and persisting despite challenges. It encourages a heightened sense of self-awareness and determination. This skill is not just important in academic scenarios but it's also essential for personal growth and building resilience, making it invaluable in all aspects of life, including professional situations and overcoming personal obstacles.
These skills are more than mere abstract ideas. In Ontario's educational system, they are quantitatively assessed, separate from typical subject grades like maths or English. They are rated on a scale from "E" for Excellent to "N" for Needs Improvement. These skills serve as fundamental pillars for the comprehensive growth of students, equipping them not only as learners but also as balanced individuals poised for success beyond the school environment. Essentially, these Learning Skills are crucial because they aim beyond mere academic achievement. They are aimed at cultivating individuals who are prepared to navigate various life challenges. This comprehensive approach to development makes Ontario's Learning Skills an invaluable facet of education.
